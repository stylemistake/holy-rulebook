FAITH POWERS AND HOW TO GET THEMUSING FAITH
16
Faith powers are abilities that are sometimes displayed by Clerics or Battle Sisters of pure faith. While sometimes they look similar to psyker powers, in reality they are a combination of motivation, placebo, adrenaline, and belief. The link to the warp also cannot be denied, and some adepts theorize that clerics are actively drawing from and projecting the power of the Emperor.The main difference between normal Talents and Faith Talents is that to use a Faith Talent a character must spend a point of Fate, tapping into their destiny and expending it to touch the divine. Using a Faith Talent also requires taking an action. This means to use a Faith Talent a character needs to A) Use either a Full Action, Half Action or Reaction as detailed in the Talents description. If no action is specified then using the Talent requires a Half Action, and B) Spend a Fate Point. Once these two requirements are met then the Faith Talent will manifest its power as detailed within the Talent description.

All Faith powers are represented as Talents, granting a character a selection of unique abilities which he can use during play. Characters also acquire these Talents by spending experience points as they would other advances. Each Faith Talent costs 400xp. It is worth noting that all Faith Talents require the character to first have Pure Faith Talent, without which the powers of faith are completely out of their reach.

DURATION
24
As suits its nature, Faith is not something that is easily or exactly quantified into hours, minutes or seconds. When a miracle occurs it can last but a moment or persist for days. To reflect this, unless otherwise specified within the Talentâ€™s description, all Faith Talent will last for one encounter/ combat. When a combat or encounter is over is always decided by the GM, though usually it will be fairly obvious as battles end or the Acolytes move on to another part of the adventure. The exception to this broad rule for durations are Faith Talents which either inflict damage or heal wounds (or other maladies) which will typically last only for an instant and end once the effect has been resolved, i.e. the damage dealt or the wounds healed. As always however the GM can decide to have a Talent last longer or end sooner based on the situation. After all, these are Emperor-given gifts and just as He bestows them He can take them away...

USING FAITH
26
A character with more than 10 Corruption Points may not use any Faith Talents and may not acquire new Faith Talents. If, through some means, they reduce their number of Corruption Points to below 10, they may again use and acquire Faith Talents.

TECH POWERS AND HOW TO GET THEM
54
Tech powers are abilities that Tech-priests eventually gain due to their replaced flesh. They are not possible without a heavily modified body, and thus only people with Mechanicus Implants may gain these powers. Due to their mechanical nature, some machines may also have these powers.
