UPGRADE RULES
87
Modifications can be made when first acquiring an armor, or later a character (or an ally) can try to apply them himself.
88
Mod limit. An armor can have a total of three modifications. Poor quality armor can only have 2 modifications. Best quality armor can have 4 modifications. An armor cannot have two modifications that share the same type (such as a scope). In that case, first mod has to be removed before installing the second. If some modifications can gain mods themselves, they share this limit with their parent item.
89
Applying modifications. Once a character has the parts for an item modification, he can use the Trade (Armourer) skill to apply that modification to the desired item . This test receives a modifier equal to the Availability of the customisation being applied (Average = 0, Scarce = -10, ect., not modified by faction bonus). If the test succeeds, the modification is successfully applied. If the test fails, the modification is not applied, and the parts necessary to apply the modification are lost in the process. Characters who suffer two or more degrees of failure damage their item, and it becomes Clumsy(1). If the item was previously Clumsy, increase it's Heavy value by 1 (or set it to 4 if no Heavy value was present).
90
Removing and re-using mods. A character can also recover the parts necessary to apply a modification by removing that modification from a different item . To do this, the character makes a Tech-Use test with a modifier equal to the Availability of the customisation being removed. If the test succeeds, the modification is successfully removed with its parts intact. If the test fails, the modification is removed, but the parts necessary to reapply the modification are lost in the process, effectively destroying the mod. Characters who suffer two or more degrees of failure damage their item, and it becomes Clumsy(1). If the item was previously Clumsy, increase it's Heavy value by 1 (or set it to 4 if no Heavy value was present).

RestrictedA faction considers this item to be one of their symbols, and restrict's the items usage to it's own agents. Some items are so valued that the faction may declare it's unauthorised users thiefs, or even heathens; on other cases these items may be displayed to display alignment, and therefore gain approval. Some other items are the opposite - they are heretical to use, and factions may react violently to their users. Effect: both related and unrelated people may link this item to a specific faction. This requires a Common Lore test. This test is easier to the members of the faction. The test is harder for outsiders in case of rare or obscure items. The outcome of recognising the item is up to GM.

Power Armor (X)Power Armor has to be powered to be used. It is powered by power cells, unless otherwise specified. Once the power cell is drained, the Power Armor becomes unpowered, which has negative effects. This lasts until the cells are replaced, which takes time. When active, Power Armor grants the wearer +10 bonus to Strength Characteristic for each Power Armour(X) value, and has its weight reduced to 0. You must be wearing the body, both arms, and both legs of power armour to benefit from these bonuses; the helmet is not required.
Power Cells:
1. Standart Power Cell duration is 5 hours. This duration is stated for combat activity (any activity that is more complicated than walking). If the PA user is engaged in elementary activities (such as walking), that cell duration is extended twice (ex. from 5 to 10 hours).
2. Standart PA Power Cells are equivalent to Heavy power packs. Recharge: 24h in fire (with standart fail rules), 3h on standart electricity supply.
3. Military supply: lasts 2x longer. Recharge: 12h in fire (with standart fail rules), 1.5h on standart electricity supply. Availability drops by 1 step (becomes very rare), and even lower in non-military stores.
Unpowered Power Armor:
If the armor becomes unpowered, the wearer must make an Hard (20) Strength Test simply to move. If he passes the test, his Base Movement is still reduced to 1, and all physical actions suffer a 20 penalty.

At GM discretion, some situations (like wearing strictly ceremonial or very tight armor) may provoke an Awareness test to notice the armor. Note that the head piece might have to be removed at all times to enable this.

Hermetic (X)The suit is able to completely isolate the wearer from the environment. The wearer gains a +10 bonus per every point of Hermetic(X) to resist environmental heat effects, cold effects, and vacuum. The wearer gains immunity from vacuum of space on Hermetic(3) or higher. He also gains a +10 bonus (not increased by X value) to resist catching on fire, and never gets wet. To receive any of these bonuses, all worn body pieces have to have the Hermetic quality. Hermetic quality can be disabled rather quickly by removing the helmet.
114
Heavy (X)The armor is extremely heavy and encumbering. If the wearer's Strength Bonus is lower than the Heavy(X) value, reduce the wearers Agility Bonus when determining movement speed by 1 for every point of difference. A single body piece is enough to reach this effect, and only the highest Heavy quality of any armor worn is in effect.
115
Large (X)The armor very large. It increases the wearers size by the Large(X) value. Larger characters are easier to hit and to detect, but they also move faster.
116
NonflammableArmour is suited to resist heat, and non-flammable materials were selected during its construction. The wearer gains a +20 bonus to agility tests to avoid catching on fire and to put the flames out. A single body piece is enough to reach this effect, and multiple nonflammable qualities do not stack.
117
Warm (X)The wearer gains a +10 bonus to resist cold for every point of Warm(X). This bonus is capped to the number of locations that have items with either Warm or Hermetic qualities equipped: head, arms, body, legs. Max bonus is +40.
118
Clumsy (X)The armor is very inflexible, and compromises body balance. If the armour is equipped, all Agility tests suffer a penalty equal to 10 times the number in parenthises (ex. Clumsy(2) armor, when equipped, would impose a -20 penalty to Agility tests). This does not reduce the Agility characteristic. A single body piece is enough to reach this effect, and only the highest Clumsy quality is in effect.
